Step 4
Target Your Audience
~ 10 minutes

What to do

Tell Meta who should see your ad. Keep it simple -- start broad.

  • Location: Singapore
  • Age: 25-45
  • Interests: Financial planning, CPF, Investments, Retirement
  • Gender: All
Don't over-target 3-5 interests is the sweet spot. Adding 10+ narrows your audience and makes leads more expensive. Let Meta's algorithm find the right people.

Step 5
Set Budget & Schedule
~ 2 minutes

What to do

$5-10/day is plenty to start. Run for 3 days minimum before judging results.

  • Budget type: Daily budget
  • Amount: $5 (or $10 if comfortable)
  • Start date: Today
  • End date: 3 days from now
Steady beats spiky In 2026, Meta's algorithm rewards consistent daily spending. Don't run $50 for 1 day. Run $5/day for 10 days.

Step 6
Build Your Ad with Claude
~ 15 minutes

What to do

This is where AI does the heavy lifting. Open Claude in another tab and use Prompt #2 from the cheat sheet.

  • Upload your lead magnet cover image
  • Paste Claude's primary text into "Primary Text"
  • Paste the headline and description
  • Select CTA: "Sign Up" or "Learn More"
Image size matters Square (1080×1080) or portrait (1080×1350) works best. Meta auto-crops for all placements.

Step 7
Create the Instant Form
~ 10 minutes

What to do

This is the form that pops up inside Facebook when someone clicks your ad.

  • Click "Create Form" under Destination
  • Form Type: More Volume (fewer friction)
  • Fields: Name, Phone (auto-filled), + custom question
  • Custom question: "What's your biggest financial concern?"
Keep it under 6 fields More fields = fewer completions. The custom question qualifies the lead AND gives you a conversation starter.

Step 8
Review Everything
~ 3 minutes

What to do

Before hitting publish, double-check every setting. Meta shows you a summary of your campaign -- walk through it together.

  • Objective matches your goal
  • Audience size is 100K+ (not too narrow)
  • Budget is set correctly
  • Ad preview looks good on mobile
  • Form is linked to the ad
No landing page? Good. Instant Forms don't need one. If you see a "landing page URL" field, it's optional.

Your first Meta ad campaign is live. Congratulations.

Meta will review your ad within 15-60 minutes. You'll see "In Review" status — that's normal. Once approved, it starts running.

After Launch

First 24 Hours

Don't change anything. Let Meta's algorithm learn. Just verify the status moves from "In Review" to "Active".

After 48 Hours

Check your results. Download leads. Follow up using Claude prompt #3. Speed wins.

CPL Under $10?

Scale it. Increase budget by 20%. Don't double it overnight -- incremental changes keep the algorithm stable.

CPL Too High?

Edit the ad copy or image. Don't delete the campaign. Let it re-optimize with the new creative.
