First, the concept

What is a lead magnet?

A free guide you trade for contact details.

  • A stranger won't book a call from an ad. Too big an ask.
  • But they will take a free PDF that solves a problem they have.
  • To get it, they give you their name and phone number.
  • That's a lead. Someone who raised their hand about a money problem.And you're the advisor who helped them first, before asking for anything.
This is the value-first mindset. You give before you ask. The PDF does the trust-building an ad can't. Keep this under 3 minutes.

Module 3 · 25 minLaptops closed, just watch

Meta ads 101

How it actually works, in plain English.

Laptops closed or lids half down. If they build while you explain, they miss the concepts and then get lost in Module 4. 25 minutes, keep moving.

The structure

Think of a filing cabinet

Campaign = the drawer. Your goal: get leads.
Ad set = the folder. Who sees it, and how much you spend.
Ad = the document. What they actually see in their feed.

You create one campaign, one ad set, one ad. That's the whole build.

Meta's interface looks intimidating because it's built for agencies running hundreds of ads. You need exactly one of each level. Repeat: drawer, folder, document.

The inputs

Meta needs 3 things from you

WHO

Who should see your ad. Targeting.

WHAT

What they see. Your image and copy.

HOW MUCH

Your daily budget.

Everything else is details. Get these 3 right and you get leads.

This slide kills overwhelm. Every screen they see in Module 4 answers one of these questions. Refer back to this: "which of the 3 is this screen asking about?"

The format

Why we use lead form ads

Traffic ad (the old way)

See ad → click → your website → find the form → type everything → maybe submit

Lead form ad (our way)

See ad → click → form opens inside Facebook, name and number already filled in → done

  • No website needed
  • Fewer steps, so more people finish
  • Facebook fills in their details for them
This is why beginners can do this today: no landing page to build, no web developer, no domain. The form lives inside Facebook.

Targeting

Who sees your ad

Location: Singapore
Age: 25-45younger for savings topics, older for retirement
Interests: financial planning, investments, CPF, retirement, insurancepick 3-5 that match your topic

Start broad. Meta's algorithm finds the right people inside your audience. Every extra restriction makes each lead more expensive.

Beginners want to over-target ("35-37 year old mothers in Tampines"). Small audiences cost more per lead. Trust the algorithm with a broad audience and let it learn.

Money

Budget reality check

Daily budget: $5-10 is plenty to start
Cost per lead in Singapore: $3-15depends on offer and audience
Minimum test: 3 days. Never judge after day 1.
Total for your first test: $15-30

Less than a nice dinner, for real leads on your phone. You can pause anytime, and you only pay when people see the ad.

Money worry is the biggest silent objection in the room. Name it directly. $15-30 total, pausable anytime, no contract. Then transition: "Time to build. Laptops open."

Ad rejected?

Normal for financial services. Remove words like "guaranteed" or "100%", any income claims. Edit and resubmit. Not a failure.

Still "In Review" tonight?

Also normal. Finance ads get extra checks. Leave it alone; most approve within a few hours.

Pre-empt the two panic messages you'd otherwise get tonight. Rejection is a policy filter, not a verdict on them. Then straight into Module 5: "so what do you do when the leads arrive?"

The rules

Four rules that save your first campaign

The 48-hour rule

Don't touch the ad for 48 hours. Meta's algorithm is learning; every edit resets it.

When to kill

48 hours + 500 impressions + zero leads: change the copy or image and relaunch. Edit, don't delete.

When to scale

Cost per lead under $10 for 3+ days: raise the budget 20%. Don't double it overnight.

Rejected?

Remove "guaranteed", "100%", income claims. Edit, resubmit, move on.

The 48-hour rule is the one they'll break. They'll check at 10pm, see 2 dollars spent and no leads, and want to change everything. Say it twice: leave it alone for 48 hours.
