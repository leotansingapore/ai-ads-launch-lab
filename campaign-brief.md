## Campaign Concepts

### Concept 1: The Cold Call Killer

**Hypothesis:** Young FAs viscerally hate cold calling -- leading with the pain of rejection and wasted hours will stop the scroll because it mirrors their daily frustration.

**Primary Message:** Stop cold calling strangers. Build a system that makes qualified prospects come to you -- in one afternoon.

**Tone:** Direct 9/10, bold 8/10, rational 7/10. No sugarcoating. The copy should feel like a senior advisor telling you the truth your manager won't.

**Visual Direction:** Dark navy (#1a1a2e) background with a stark visual metaphor: one side shows a cracked, dimming phone icon (representing exhausting cold calls), the other shows a glowing gold (#e8b931) notification pulse wave representing inbound leads. Abstract, not literal -- no photos of people on phones. Minimal texture, generous negative space. The contrast should feel like "before vs. after" without words.

**Target Platforms:** Meta Feed (Facebook + Instagram) -- pain-point hooks perform well in-feed where users are passively scrolling and emotionally receptive. Stories/Reels as secondary for retargeting.

**CTA:** Reserve My Seat

**Addresses:** General brand awareness -- targets the core pain point (cold calling reliance) identified in brand-profile.json.

### Concept 2: The 1,002 Proof Stack

**Hypothesis:** In a market flooded with "gurus," Leo's verifiable credentials (5X MDRT, CFA, 1,002 clients closed, NUS Engineering) are a genuine differentiator -- leading with proof collapses skepticism and builds instant credibility.

**Primary Message:** 5X MDRT. CFA. 1,002 clients closed. Now he's showing financial advisors the exact AI ads system behind those numbers -- for free.

**Tone:** Direct 8/10, proof-driven 9/10, bold 7/10. Confident authority without arrogance. Let the numbers speak.

**Visual Direction:** Deep navy (#1a1a2e) background. A bold ascending data curve rendered in glowing gold (#e8b931) -- abstract, not a literal chart. The curve rises from lower-left to upper-right, suggesting compounding growth. Small geometric accent shapes (circles, lines) in muted gold scattered for texture. Clean, dark-mode aesthetic. No stock photos, no headshots in the generated image (headshot overlays handled separately in ad assembly). Generous negative space.

**Target Platforms:** Meta Feed (Facebook + Instagram) -- credential-led ads perform well in financial services where trust is the primary conversion barrier. Also strong for Instagram Stories where the bold visual and short proof-stack copy can be consumed in seconds.

**CTA:** Book My Free Seat

**Addresses:** General brand awareness -- leverages the credibility data from brand-profile.json to differentiate from generic marketing workshop ads.

### Concept 3: The Locked Room

**Hypothesis:** Scarcity and social proof together are the strongest conversion drivers for free event registrations -- when young FAs believe their peers are already adopting AI ads, fear of being left behind outweighs inertia.

**Primary Message:** 20 seats. Your competitors already signed up. The advisors who learn AI ads first will own the next decade of leads.

**Tone:** Bold 9/10, direct 8/10, urgent 7/10. The language should create tension -- this is not a sales pitch, it is a closing window.

**Visual Direction:** Deep navy (#1a1a2e) background. A single glowing gold (#e8b931) doorway or portal shape -- narrow, bright, centered -- representing the exclusive entry point. Surrounding the portal: abstract silhouettes or geometric shapes suggesting a crowd converging. The composition should feel like "the door is closing." Stark contrast between the bright gold entry and the dark surrounding void. Minimal, no clutter.

**Target Platforms:** Instagram Stories/Reels (primary) -- urgency and FOMO messaging converts best in ephemeral, full-screen formats where the user feels time pressure. Meta Feed as secondary for broader reach.

**CTA:** Claim My Spot

**Addresses:** General brand awareness -- uses scarcity framing to drive immediate registration action, particularly effective for free workshops where the perceived cost of inaction is low.
