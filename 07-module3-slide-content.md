## Slide 2: The Filing Cabinet

Think of Meta Ads like a filing cabinet:

- **Campaign** = The drawer (your goal -- in our case, "get leads")
- **Ad Set** = The folder (who sees it + how much you spend)
- **Ad** = The document (what they actually see in their feed)

You create one campaign. Inside it, one ad set. Inside that, one ad. Simple.

## Slide 3: Meta Needs 3 Things From You

1. **WHO** to show your ad to (targeting)
2. **WHAT** to show them (your ad creative + copy)
3. **HOW MUCH** to spend (daily budget)

That's it. Everything else is details. Get these 3 right and you'll get leads.

---

## Slide 4: Lead Form Ads vs Traffic Ads

**Traffic ad (the old way):**
User sees ad -> clicks -> goes to your website -> finds the form -> fills it in -> maybe

**Lead form ad (what we're using):**
User sees ad -> clicks -> form pops up INSIDE Facebook -> auto-fills their name and email -> done

Why lead forms win:
- No website needed
- Less friction (fewer steps = more people complete it)
- Facebook auto-fills their details (they don't even have to type)
- Perfect for beginners -- no landing page to build

## Slide 5: Targeting for Financial Advisors in Singapore

Your ad set controls who sees your ad. We'll use:

- **Location:** Singapore
- **Age:** 25-45 (adjust to your niche -- younger for savings, older for retirement)
- **Interests:** Financial planning, Investments, CPF, Retirement planning, Insurance

Pro tip: Start broad. Don't over-target. Meta's algorithm is smart -- it'll find the right people within your audience. The more you restrict, the more expensive each lead becomes.

---

## Slide 6: Budget Reality Check

- **Daily budget:** $5-10/day is plenty to start
- **Expected cost per lead:** $3-15 in Singapore (depends on your offer and audience)
- **Minimum test period:** 3 days -- don't judge results after 1 day
- **Total test cost:** $15-30 for your first 3-day test

"You're about to spend less than a nice dinner to get real leads delivered to your phone."

- You can pause or stop anytime
- You only pay when people see your ad (not when they click or fill in the form)
- Start small, learn, then scale what works
